'The Canterbury Tales' is a collection of twenty-four tales written in Middle English by Geoffrey Chaucer in the 14th century. The tales are presented as part of a story-telling contest among a group of pilgrims on their way to Canterbury. The tales are generally divided into 'Fragments'.
